### Probing Both The Philosophical and Mathematical Aspects of Determinism
In information theory and computational linguistics, determinism can be discussed through concepts such as:

1. **Entropy**: In information theory, entropy measures the uncertainty or randomness in a system. A deterministic system would have low entropy, as its outcomes are predictable.

2. **Markov Chains**: In computational linguistics, Markov chains are used to model sequences where the future state depends only on the current state (deterministic in nature). However, these models also account for probabilistic transitions, introducing a mix of determinism and randomness.

3. **Finite State Machines (FSMs)**: FSMs are computational models used in linguistics to process languages. A deterministic FSM (DFA) processes input in a predictable manner, whereas a non-deterministic FSM (NFA) allows for multiple possible states, adding an element of non-determinism.

These concepts illustrate how determinism and randomness are addressed in both fields, often blending the two to model complex systems.
